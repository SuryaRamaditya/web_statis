Cyberpunk 2077 2.0 Updates and Changes

The release of Cyberpunk 2077’s Phantom Liberty expansion also comes with an early brand-new base game update. The free Cyberpunk 2.0 update brings some massive changes to the base game that players can enjoy alongside the expansion, from a brand new perks system to improved AI. we’ve broken down each new update and change from 2.0 so you can know what to expect from Cyberpunk’s main game and its thrilling upcoming Phantom Liberty expansion.

  • Reworked Perks System
  • Vehicle Combat
  • Armor and Cyberware Changes
  • Stamina and Weapons
  • Updated Ripperdoc Interactions
  • Updated Police AI
  • General Changes

Reworked Perks System

One of the biggest changes in Cyberpunk’s 2.0 update is that the Attribute and Character Perk skill trees have been massively overhauled. It now provides much more focused perks so you can better craft a build that suits your playstyle. For those jumping into Phantom Liberty, you’ll also be introduced to a brand new Relic tree, which features a variety of exciting new perks, from Emergency Cloaking to new abilities for your arm Cyberware. Unlike the regular perk tree, the Relic tree uses specific Relic points that can be earned while in Dogtown and is exclusive to the Phantom Liberty expansion.

Some of the exciting new perks in the 2.0 update are Hack Queue, which lets you queue up multiple quick hacks at once; Air Dash, which allows you to move swiftly through the air; Quake, which lets you pulverize a group of enemies all at once; and Lead and Steel, which allows you to block incoming projectiles with a blade. Many of these new and reworked perks also feature brand-new, flashy animations, which makes them even more enjoyable.

Vehicle Combat

With the 2.0 update, you can be just as dangerous behind the wheel as you are behind the trigger. With the new vehicle-on-vehicle combat, you’re able to shoot from inside of your car for high- speed battles. And thanks to the new Carhacker perk, you can also use your cyberware to remotely hack your way into enemy vehicles. This includes messing with their brakes, going pedal to the metal with their gas, or even blowing them up.

Some cars even have mounted guns, so you can truly go wild on the streets of Night City. According to CDPR, most of the cars with mounted guns are exclusive to Phantom Liberty. However, players who own just the base game will find updated Aldecados cars fitted with mounted weaponry.

Armor and Cyberware Changes

Cyberware and armor have seen some big in the 2.0 update as well. Now, there’s a limit on how much cyberware you can have installed at one time, which is tied to your level. As you level up the amount of cyberware you can install increases.

Cyberware is also now tied to armor, a stat that originally used to be an aspect of the clothing you wore. With this change, your cyberware installments can feel like more impactful decisions that help you feel more powerful in a number of ways as you explore Night City.

Stamina and Weapons

Stamina used to be tied to your movement and melee combat, but now firing a weapon and dashing/dodging will drain it. So, while you’ll be able to traverse the world of Night City without running out of breath, this new update adds some tension to combat as you’ll now have to keep an eye on your stamina meter as you take on enemies. Updated Ripperdoc Interactions

The Ripperdocs have gotten an upgrade with the 2.0 update as well. The UI has been switched up to make choosing the cyberware you want a bit easier, and there’s also an animation now that shows you getting up from the chair after a surgery (along with a few sound effects during it to immerse you in the process). This has made interactions with Ripperdocs feel a touch more true to the Cyberpunk experience, so when you settle in for a surgery, you can feel more absorbed in the process.

Updated Police AI

With the 2.0 update, you’ll need to stay on your toes while exploring Night City as well, as the police’s AI have been significantly improved. Your illegal actions won’t slip by all that easily anymore, as the police now dynamically react to what you do. Similar to GTA, there’s an escalation system (or ‘Heat’ level) in place that increases in correlation with the level of crime you’ve committed. Commit too many crimes, and you’ll likely have quite the run-in with the NCPD, and may even end up in a chase!

Alongside the NCPD, enemies in groups will also be more aware of you and your actions. Should you push their buttons, they even adapt to V’s difficulty level, so they’ll be ready to take you on in a proper fight.

General Changes

Alongside the above-mentioned changes in the 2.0 update, there are a variety of smaller updates that have been implemented as well. Various UI improvements have been made, there are three new radio stations to jam out to, and you now start with two healing items that recharge after they’re used. This is also applied to grenades, so when you’re in combat, you’ll need to be mindful of how often you’re using them.
